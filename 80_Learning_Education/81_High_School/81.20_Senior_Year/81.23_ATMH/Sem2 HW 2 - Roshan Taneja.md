Are the vectors $(4, 5, -2)$ and $(3,-1, 5)$ orthogonal, parallel, or neither?

if dot product is zero, then vectors are perpendicular b/c $\frac{u \cdot v}{||u||*||v||} = \cos(\theta)$

$$ \begin{aligned}
&(4, 5, -2) \cdot (3,-1, 5) \\
\\
&= (4 * 3) + (5 * -1) + (-2 * 5) \\
&= 12 - 5 - 10 \\
&= -3
\end{aligned}$$
Therefore the vectors are *Not* orthogonal
